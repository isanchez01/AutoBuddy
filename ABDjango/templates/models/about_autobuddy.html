About AutoBuddy

Thank you for checking out my project, AutoBuddy! My name is Israel Sanchez, and as a car guy, I love repairing my cars. Something about getting your hands filthy with grease and hearing your car run like new after replacing that defective part makes me feel satisfied and accomplished. This feeling of satisfaction is further amplified by the gigantic savings that come with fixing vehicles at home! The $350 it takes to have a mechanic replace the valve cover gasket in your 2013 Ford Escape will merely cost $23.79 and your time. Plus, you'll get rid of that stinky smell of burning oil.

However, auto repair is a complex task to take on, especially today's cars which utilize lots of electonic parts. Not many people know how to repair their car, and learning usually entails a few repairs being botched, which can be catastrophic to the vehicle. Because of this, many people choose to pay an expert to fix their car. The auto repair industy is notorious for being an oasis of dishonest mechanics who charge customers excessively for repairs that they often comlplete poorly, if they complete them at all. The internet is full of stories of unfortunate indivudals who fell victim to a malicious mechanic. AutoBuddy hopes to curtail this problem by providing users with the ability to look up the average price for a repair or set of repairs for their specific car. The idea behind retrieving the average price for repairs is to provide users with a reference point. They can compare the price that they received to this reference point, and in turn, see if they're being charged reasonably.

About AutoBuddy

The idea for AutoBuddy came to me after experimenting with a few auto repair estimators.
As I used these estimators, I became inspired to create my own, however, I would add my own spins on it.
Instead of pulling numbers from external sources, AutoBuddy's estimates are calculated from reports that are
submitted by actual users.

AutoBuddy also offers users a tool to check if a quote they got from the shop is reasonably priced.
The Is It Fair tool works the same way as the AutoBuddy Estimator, except that the tool presents the
user with a graphical colored spectrum that'll help users with determining the fairness of their quote.

The goal that AutoBuddy strives to acheive is to help users save money on auto repair by giving them the tools
to avoid being taken advantage of from high auto repair prices.

AutoBuddy Estimator

As mentioned in the About AutoBuddy section, the AutoBuddy Estimator takes the car and repair info submitted
by the user, and sends it to an API that will calculate the estimate for the part(s) sent by the user.
AutoBuddy uses a Postgreql database to store information on cars, users, and reports, which will later be used
to calculate the estimate.

The user will be asked to select his/her vehicle's year, make, and model from three
dropdown buttons. Upon clicking the "submit" button, he/she will be presented with the estimated total cost of
repair. If multiple repairs were selected, then the estimated costs of each repair will also be displayed.

Is It Fair

The Is It Fair tool acts kind of like a shield to users in that it can protect them from overpriced auto repairs.
Its primary purpose is to compare the user-submitted costs for specific repairs to their averages calculated by the API.
The tool would then display a colored spectrum that will notify the user whether or not they're paying too much.

The Is It Fair spectrum will display one of four colors to the user, depending on how cheap or expensive the quote is
relative to the calculated average. The four colors are: Dark Green, Green, Yellow, and Red.

Dark Green indicates that the user's quote for their submitted repairs is significantly below the calculated average, and that the user is getting
one heck of a deal!

Green indicates that the user's quote is either slightly below or at the calculated average, and is either getting
somewhat of a deal, or is at least getting charged the average price.

Yellow indicates that the user's quote is slightly above the calculated average price. The user is kind of paying
more than they should, but they are certainly not getting their head knocked off.

Red indicates that the user's quote is significantly above the calculated average and that he/she should probably
find a different mechanic.

Making A Report

If you've recently taken your car to your local mechanic and are felling particularly helpful, feel free to send
a report to AutoBuddy!

To send AutoBuddy a report of your auto repair costs, click on the "Make a Report!" button.
You will be prompted to enter in your first and last name, along with your vehicle's information
just like you would in the AutoBuddy Estimator and Is It Fair? tools.
